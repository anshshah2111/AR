# Week-by-Week Timeline (26 Weeks)

A realistic sequence assuming the festival opens at Month 6 (Week 24–26). Adjust all dates relative to your festival target dates.

## PHASE 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

- [ ] Begin Fractured Atlas fiscal sponsorship application
- [ ] Direct outreach to Anjani Saraf (India) for letter of support and India-side intelligence
- [ ] Draft festival concept note (if not already done)

### Week 2
- [ ] Fractured Atlas approval expected (or identify alternative fiscal sponsor)
- [ ] Meeting with CGI Seattle (ideally in person)
- [ ] Meeting with CGI SF (in person or Zoom)

### Week 3

## Critical Path Summary

The 4 items that cannot slip:

1. **Week 1–2: Consulate meetings booked.** Everything else downstream depends on this.
2. **Week 4: Fiscal sponsor confirmed + artists identified.** Can't file visas without these.
3. **Week 8: I-129 petitions filed.** The visa timeline is the hardest deadline in the whole plan.
4. **Week 14: Artists have visas stamped.** Festival fails without this.

Everything else has wiggle room. These four don't.

---

## Go / No-Go Gates

| Week | Gate | If No-Go |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Fiscal sponsor live + co-presenter confirmed | Pause and reassess; can't proceed without US legal structure |
| 8 | At least 2 major funders committed + I-129 filed | Scale down to 3 artists from 5; consider postponing by 2 months |
| 14 | All artists have US visas | Substitute artists, or postpone event by ~30 days |
| 20 | Ticket sales at 50% of target | Intensify community outreach; renegotiate venue if possible |
