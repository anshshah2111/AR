# Seattle Local Advantages — The Founder's Playbook

This file captures the specific, concrete actions that become possible when the founding team is Seattle-based with strong existing Indian community ties. This is not strategy — it's tactics. Treat it as a week-by-week field manual for the local lead.

---

## The Three Unfair Advantages

### 1. In-Person Access to CGI Seattle
The Consulate is at the Federal Reserve Building, 1015 2nd Avenue. You can physically show up. You can hand-deliver a concept note. You can attend their public events and introduce yourself at the reception. Remote teams cannot do any of this.

### 2. A Pre-Built Community Network
Indian community relationships in Seattle are already warm. You don't have to cold-pitch trust. You have volunteer recruitment, word-of-mouth marketing, and community advisory capacity that a Bay Area or LA-based team would take months to build.

### 3. Walk-In Venue Scouting
Every potential Seattle venue (Benaroya, McCaw, Seattle Center, Seattle U, UW venues) is within a 30-minute drive. You can do walk-through scouting in a single day. You can build venue-manager relationships by showing up and being known.

## Relationship-First Before the Ask

The single biggest mistake a local founder makes is emailing cold before showing up. **The sequence is always: show up → be seen → introduce → ask.** Do this for every Tier 1 Seattle stakeholder.

### Before the First CGI Seattle Email (Weeks 1–2)

- Check [indiainseattle.gov.in](https://indiainseattle.gov.in) and Facebook (`@IndiaInSeattle`) for upcoming public events
- Attend 1–2 public events (Republic Day, Independence Day, cultural events, book talks, yoga sessions)
- Introduce yourself briefly at the reception — *"Hi, I'm [name], working on an India-US performing arts initiative — I'd love to request a formal meeting in the coming weeks"*
- Collect business cards or LinkedIn connections from anyone from the Consulate staff you meet
- The goal is not to pitch; it is to be recognized. When your email lands, it is not cold.

### Before the First Venue Outreach

- Walk in to Benaroya Hall and Seattle Center as a regular visitor — note acoustics, capacity, crowd flow
- Ask for a brochure at each venue's front desk; get the venue manager's name and email
- Attend one public performance at each venue to see how it feels
- Your venue outreach email becomes *"I attended [performance] at Benaroya last month and the [room] feels like it would be a strong match for our festival..."* — specific, informed, warm

### Before Community Outreach

- Make a list of the 15–25 most connected people in your existing network
- Schedule coffee or short calls with 3–5 of them in the first two weeks
- Frame these as listening sessions, not asks: *"I'm exploring this initiative — I want to understand what you've seen work and not work in Seattle's Indian performing arts space"*
- The people who hear your idea early and contribute to it become your ambassadors

## Events to Attend (Seattle Indian Community Calendar)

Attending these is the most cost-effective community-engagement work you can do. Each one has a specific strategic purpose.

### Consulate & Government Events

- **Republic Day Reception (January 26)** — CGI Seattle hosts a major reception. Everyone who matters in the Seattle Indian community is there. Go every year.
- **Independence Day (August 15)** — Similar scale. Consulate's highest-profile event of the year.
- **ICCR Foundation Day** — April. Consulate sometimes hosts.
- **Any Swami Vivekananda Monument event at Westlake** — ICCR-connected gatherings, prime networking

### Community & Cultural Events

- **Diwali: Lights of India at Seattle Center (annual, October)** — Organized by Northwest Share. 30+ Indian performing arts groups in one day. This is the single most important event to attend for festival planning. Every local Indian performing arts producer, dancer, musician, teacher, and community organizer is there.
- **Seattle Center Festál programs throughout the year** — Ongoing cultural festivals including Diwali, plus other Asian cultural celebrations
- **UW and Seattle University India-related lectures and performances** — Academic partners' programming; shows what kinds of institutional programming already happens

### Temple & Religious Community Events

- **Seattle-area temples and Gurudwaras** — Vedic Cultural Center, Hindu Temple and Cultural Center of Bothell, Bothell Gurudwara (Consul General Gupta has visited). Showing up at major holiday celebrations builds goodwill.

### Professional & Business Networks

- **India Association of Western Washington events** — Diaspora professional networking
- **TiE Seattle events** — Indian-American entrepreneur network
- **Microsoft, Amazon, TCS, Infosys cultural employee events** — Often openly accessible; potential sponsor pathway

## Tactical Playbook for Weeks 1–4

### Week 1 — Soft Launch

- Do **one coffee meeting per day** with a member of your existing Seattle network. Listen. Take notes.
- Identify upcoming CGI Seattle public event in the next 2–4 weeks; mark it on the calendar
- Draft the festival concept note — share it with 2–3 trusted community members for feedback
- Begin Fractured Atlas application
- Draft the CGI Seattle meeting request email — do not send yet

### Week 2 — First Consulate Touch

- **Attend the CGI Seattle public event.** Introduce yourself briefly at the reception. Leave a business card with any consulate staff you meet.
- **Send the CGI Seattle meeting request email** the day after the event: *"I was at the [event] last night — thank you for hosting. As discussed with [name if applicable], I'd like to request a formal meeting to discuss..."*
- Visit Benaroya Hall and Seattle Center in person — do walk-through scouting
- Reach out to Sital Kalantry at Roundglass India Center — reference your Seattle community involvement
- 2–3 more community coffee meetings

### Week 3 — First Formal Meeting

- CGI Seattle meeting (ideally happens this week)
- Immediately after meeting: send thank-you note; confirm action items; request specific endorsements for ICCR and visa work
- Meeting with Seattle University Office of Global Engagement (warm intro via CGI Seattle)
- Meeting with UW South Asia Center
- Begin outreach to Indian performing arts community in Seattle for Day 3 community showcase collaboration

### Week 4 — Lock Seattle Framework

- Fractured Atlas approval expected
- Provisional festival dates locked
- Seattle primary venue confirmed (at least in principle)
- 3–5 candidate Indian artists identified
- First Bay Area scouting trip planned for Week 5

## Leveraging Your Community Network Strategically

Your existing Seattle Indian community relationships are worth dollars-on-the-budget. Here's how to convert them.

### Community Advisory Circle
Identify 5–8 respected community members (not the same as donors or sponsors — these are credibility givers) and ask them to serve as an informal Community Advisory Circle. No committee structure needed; just their name listed on festival materials adds gravity.

### Volunteer Corps
From your network, recruit 10–15 volunteers across:
- Box office / check-in
- Green room / hospitality
- Stage management assistance
- Social media / content
- Photography
- Translation / artist liaison

These are each $500–$2,000 worth of contractor work per person per event. On a 4-day festival, volunteer capacity is a $10K–$20K budget savings.

### Word-of-Mouth Marketing
Your community network is the marketing engine. Before the public announcement (Week 11 in the timeline), pre-announce to 20–30 community leaders and ask them to share with their networks when public announcement goes live. This drives organic ticket sales.

### Local Artists as Community Showcase
Day 3's community showcase at Seattle Center can feature local Seattle Indian performing arts groups alongside visiting Indian masters. This:
- Honors the existing Seattle Indian arts community
- Creates a lineage narrative (local → international)
- Builds goodwill with local institutions that might otherwise see the festival as competitive
- Gives the festival a community-first flavor that pure "imported Indian artists" festivals lack

## Visiting Bay Area — Trip Planning

Given the team is Seattle-based, Bay Area trips need to be efficient. Three strategic trips during the 6-month timeline:

### Trip 1: Week 2 — Relationship-Building
- CGI SF meeting with Consul General Reddy's office
- Visit Center for the Performing Arts, San Jose (walk-through if accessible)
- Meet with Roundglass India Center Bay Area contact if any
- Coffee with 2–3 Bay Area Indian-American community leaders identified through your network
- Duration: 2–3 days

### Trip 2: Week 5 — Venue & Partner Confirmation
- Lock Bay Area flagship venue
- Meeting with Abhinaya Dance Company or other potential Indian-arts co-presenter
- Meet Ali Akbar College of Music faculty (potential expert letter writers)
- Corporate sponsor meetings (Adobe, LinkedIn, Google)
- Duration: 3 days

### Trip 3: Week 20–22 — Pre-Production
- Final venue walk-through with technical team
- Press visit / media outreach
- Check-in with CGI SF
- Lodging and hospitality logistics
- Duration: 2–3 days

**Total Bay Area travel: 7–9 days across the 6-month timeline.** Manageable for a small team.

## What to Skip for Year 1 (Protect Team Capacity)

Given the 2–3 person team size, explicitly deprioritize:

- Los Angeles outreach beyond a single polite introduction email to CGI LA
- Media coverage in non-festival cities (New York, DC)
- Major film/media component
- Visual arts exhibition
- Post-festival touring to additional cities
- Building a standalone festival website in-house (use Fractured Atlas's built-in tools plus a simple Webflow or Squarespace)
- Trying to establish a formal 501(c)(3) in Year 1 (fiscal sponsorship handles this)

Say no to these now, in writing, so that mid-timeline pressure doesn't tempt you to say yes later.

### 2-Person Team
- **Lead / Artistic + Institutional Relations** (consulate, ICCR, artist curation, sponsor relationships)
- **Producer / Operations** (venues, production, visa logistics, fiscal sponsor, budget)

### 3-Person Team (ideal)
- **Lead / Artistic Direction + Consulate Relations**
- **Producer / Operations + Budget**
- **Community & Marketing** (local network activation, press, community programming, volunteers)

Whoever takes the "Community & Marketing" role should ideally be the most connected person in your Seattle Indian community network.

## Critical Reminders for the Seattle Founder

1. **Show up before you ask.** Every time.
2. **Hand-deliver the concept note** to the Consulate if you can get past the security desk during a public event.
3. **Say yes to small speaking opportunities** in your community before the festival — 5 minutes at a community dinner, a mention in a local podcast, a guest slot at a temple cultural committee meeting.
4. **Don't let Seattle comfort make you slow.** The urgency of the 6-month timeline applies regardless of where you live.
5. **Protect the Bay Area trips.** Those 7–9 travel days are non-negotiable — the flagship evening will not plan itself remotely.
