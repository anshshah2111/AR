# Precedent Research: What Has Been Done

Before inventing anything, look at what exists. These are the events, partnerships, and institutional initiatives that prove a path forward. Each one teaches us something.

For structured/filterable data, see [`data/precedents.json`](../data/precedents.json).

## Seattle / Pacific Northwest

### India Culture Week at Seattle University (November 12–14, 2024)
**The single most important precedent for us.**

- **Partners:** CGI Seattle + Seattle University Office of Global Engagement + Roundglass India Center
- **Scope:** 3-day multi-disciplinary program — Indian classical and folk dance, food festival, yoga, arts workshops, photo exhibit, library gift
- **Headline performance:** "Natyam: A Dance Mosaic of Bharat" by the Prashanthi Chitre Institute of Performing Arts
- **Scale:** Sold-out inaugural celebration; immersive format
- **Lead speakers:** Consul General Prakash Gupta, SU President Eduardo Peñalver, Sital Kalantry (Roundglass India Center Executive Director)

**What this teaches us:**
- CGI Seattle actively wants to replicate this format
- A US university is the institutional anchor that makes it work
- Roundglass India Center is the proven intermediary
- The "multi-disciplinary India Week" format is now a known template
- **Our opening pitch to CGI Seattle should explicitly reference India Culture Week 2024 and propose being the next iteration — scaled up into a festival.**

### Swami Vivekananda Monument Unveiling (April 11, 2026)
- **Partners:** ICCR + City of Seattle + CGI Seattle
- **Scale:** City-government level diplomatic cultural gift
- **Sculptor:** Naresh Kumar Kumawat (Indian sculptor with 600+ installations in 80+ countries)
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates ICCR gifts cultural monuments/assets to US cities with credible co-presenters. Shows the scale of ICCR's West Coast investment.

### Diwali: Lights of India (Annual, Seattle Center)
- **Producer:** Northwest Share (umbrella organization for cultural festivals)
- **Partners:** City of Seattle, 4Culture, Seattle Center Festál
- **Scope:** 30+ Indian performing arts groups in one day — Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi, Manipuri, folk forms from Kerala, Maharashtra, and beyond
- **Model:** Free community festival; strong municipal backing
- **Why it matters:** Shows the density of Indian performing arts groups already operating in Seattle. Also shows that 4Culture and Seattle Center are experienced funders and venues for Indian programming.

### Indian Consulate Hosts First Baisakhi at Washington State Capitol (April 2025)
- **Partners:** CGI Seattle + Washington State legislature
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates state-government access. For us, this means state-level proclamations or recognitions are achievable asks.

### India Showcased at Asia Pacific Cultural Center, Tacoma (March 2025)
- **Partners:** CGI Seattle + APCC
- **Model:** "Theme country" showcase
- **Why it matters:** Tacoma venue option if Seattle proper is full; APCC is an experienced Asian-cultural presenter

### Seattle Symphony — "Songs and Dances of India" (2024–25 season)
- **Program:** Celebrate Asia project
- **Scope:** Symphony programming including Reena Esmail compositions and Bollywood classics
- **Why it matters:** Major US classical institution is already open to Indian programming. Seattle Symphony is a potential co-presenter for a festival classical music component.

### First India Day Parade, Seattle (August 2025)
- Showcased all 28 Indian states
- **Why it matters:** Consulate investment in pan-Indian cultural diversity, not just Bollywood/Hindi-centric programming

## Bay Area / California

### Ali Akbar College of Music (since 1967)
- Founded by legendary sarod master Ali Akbar Khan in Berkeley, relocated to San Rafael
- Oldest Hindustani classical music institution in North America
- **Why it matters:** Institutional foundation we can partner with; potential artist source, faculty as experts for P-3 visa support letters

### The Music Circle, Los Angeles (since 1973)
- Founded by Ravi Shankar and Harihar Rao
- Multiple ACTA Living Cultures grants (2010, 2012, 2014, 2020+)
- Organizes North and South Indian classical music seasons
- **Why it matters:** Direct precedent for a fundable Indian classical music programming model. If LA becomes a Year 2 anchor, The Music Circle is a top co-presenter candidate.

### CGI SF Swami Vivekananda Musical Tribute (July 26, 2025)
- **Venue:** Center for the Performing Arts, San Jose
- **Partners:** CGI SF + Vedanta Society, Berkeley
- **Artists:** Dr. L. Subramaniam, Pt. Swapan Chaudhuri, Rakesh Chaurasia, Ashwini Bhide Deshpande, Ambi Subramanian — a top-tier slate
- **Why it matters:** Proves CGI SF will activate major venues (San Jose CPA) and top artists. Directly replicable for us — this is our aspirational format.

### Pandit Chitresh Das / Chhandam School of Kathak
- Bay Area Kathak school, international reputation
- Das passed in 2015; his legacy continues through the Chitresh Das Dance Company
- **Why it matters:** Major Kathak institution; potential artist/faculty partner

## National Models

### Cleveland Thyagaraja Aradhana Festival (40 years of annual programming)
- Community-run Carnatic music festival
- Has used P-3 artist visas repeatedly — demonstrates that even small dance schools with minimal staff can successfully petition for Indian artists
- **Why it matters:** De-risks the P-3 pathway. If a small dance school in Cleveland can do it, so can we.

### Indian Performing Art Center, California
- California Arts Council grantee
- State-funded Indian performing arts organization
- **Why it matters:** Precedent for a state arts council funding Indian-specific performing arts programming

### Indian Fine Arts Academy of San Diego
- IRS 501(c)(3) organization
- **Why it matters:** Another potential institutional partner if San Diego ever enters scope

## What All These Precedents Tell Us

1. **CGI Seattle is the most active consulate on the West Coast for cultural programming.** Their preferred format is multi-day, multi-disciplinary, free-to-attend, and university-anchored.

2. **CGI SF will activate top-tier artists and major venues** when the partner and proposal are serious. They work through established cultural institutions (Vedanta Society, universities).

3. **ICCR invests where there is a credible US co-presenter.** Monuments, scholarships, travel grants — all flow through the consulate, but the consulate needs a US partner holding the other end.

4. **The multi-city/state approach has precedent.** CGI Seattle engages multiple state governors; their programming reach is regional, not just Seattle-city-limits.

5. **University partnerships are the unlock.** Seattle University for Week 2024; UW for meetings; UC Berkeley and Stanford for Bay Area.

6. **The P-3 visa pathway is well-tested.** Cleveland, Dallas, and dozens of other US cities run annual Indian classical festivals with imported Indian artists. We are not pioneering this.

7. **Corporate sponsorship is real.** CGI Seattle has met with Amazon VP for International Public Policy. CGI SF has direct access to Indian-American Bay Area tech leaders.

8. **Multiple funding streams can stack.** Diwali at Seattle Center combines 4Culture + City of Seattle + Seattle Center Foundation + private sponsorship. We can do the same.
