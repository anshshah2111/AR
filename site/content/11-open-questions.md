# Open Questions

These are the questions that need answers before we can sharpen the plan from research to execution. Each one has a default assumption — feel free to override.

---

## Strategic

### 1. Festival Dates
What window are we targeting in Month 5–6?
- Default assumption: late in the 6-month window (late September or early October, if we start in April 2026)
- Consideration: avoid overlap with major Indian festivals (Diwali season, Ganesh Chaturthi) unless explicitly tied
- Consideration: avoid Seattle Center Festál dates if we want independence

### 2. Thematic Focus
Is there a theme for Year 1, or is it a general showcase?
- Options: "Classical Traditions: Masters and Rising Stars" / "Regional Voices of India" / specific anniversary tie-in
- Default assumption: "Masters and Rising Stars" cross-generational pairing

### 3. Indian Focal Sponsor
- What is the sponsor's contribution range?
- Is it structured as a grant, sponsorship, or services contribution?
- Do they have a US affiliate or bank relationship?
- Are there branding/naming rights they expect?

### 4. Anjani Saraf's Specific Role
- Is she providing a letter of support, an introduction, a funding contribution, or an institutional connection?
- Is there a specific Indian government ministry or institution she can open doors with?
- We should defer her outreach until the concept note is polished enough to share.

## Operational

### 5. Team & Capacity
- Who is the festival lead / decision maker?
- Is there paid staff in place, or is this largely founder-driven?
- What production expertise exists on the team?

### 6. Existing Relationships
- Does anyone on the team already have a warm contact at:
  - CGI Seattle or CGI SF
  - A major Bay Area or Seattle venue
  - An Indian corporate or foundation with cultural philanthropy focus
  - A major Indian classical artist or artist manager

### 7. Artist Curation Authority
- Who has final artistic authority?
- Are artists curated via formal advisory panel, or single curator?
- Is there an existing shortlist?

---

## Legal & Financial

### 8. Sponsor Structure Decision
- Plan assumes: Fractured Atlas fiscal sponsorship (fastest path)
- Confirm: Is this acceptable? Or is there a preferred alternative?

### 9. Revenue Split
- If we co-present with Roundglass, The Music Circle, or any Indian-arts 501(c)(3), how is revenue split?
- If we use an Indian focal sponsor, what are their expectations around brand placement and revenue share?

### 10. Insurance & Liability
- Event insurance is a must for festival-scale events
- Worker's comp for contractors
- Who handles this — fiscal sponsor or direct?

## Creative & Content

### 11. Programming Details
- Do we want a signature opening number / collaboration piece that brings multiple artists together?
- Is there a commissioned work angle?
- Do we want a lecture-demonstration by a senior master as a marquee educational event?

### 12. Documentation
- Are we filming/recording performances for:
  - Artistic archive
  - Grant reporting
  - Future streaming / on-demand
  - Documentary purposes
- Rights structure with artists needs to reflect this

### 13. Merchandise / Publications
- Program book at $15–$25 is a revenue line item
- Festival-branded merchandise possible
- Artist CDs/recordings for sale?

## Marketing

### 14. Branding & Naming
- Working title "India–USA Performing Arts Festival" is descriptive, not distinctive
- Consider a Sanskrit or Hindi word as a branded name (e.g., "Sangam" = confluence, "Kala" = art, "Raag" = melody, "Anand" = joy)
- Default assumption: pilot year can run as "Inaugural India–USA Performing Arts Festival" and rebrand for Year 2

### 15. Web Presence
- Domain name to register
- Website timeline (should be live by Week 11, when tickets go on sale)

### 16. Community Ambassadors
- Known Indian-American community leaders willing to lend name to the festival
- Could include: former diplomats, prominent Indian-American business leaders, existing Indian-arts patrons

---

## Risks to Plan For

### 17. Visa Denial Contingency
- If one or more artists' visas are denied or delayed, do we have substitute artists ready?
- Having at least 2 backup artists on the shortlist is prudent

### 18. Venue Contingency
- If primary venue falls through, what's the backup?
- Secondary venues in each city should be in conversation

### 19. Funding Shortfall Contingency
- If total funding lands at $150K instead of $225K, which line items get cut?
- Suggested priority: cut ambitious programming extras before cutting artist fees or travel

### 20. Political / External Events
- US-India political or diplomatic incidents could affect consulate availability
- Festival should be politically neutral and brand-agnostic
