# Executive Summary

## The Initiative

A **Seattle-led bi-coastal pilot festival** of Indian performing arts (classical music, classical dance, and theater) staged primarily in **Seattle** (where the founding team is based) with a **flagship single-evening event in the San Francisco Bay Area** within a 6-month horizon. Year 1 is a pilot that builds institutional foundations and proves the model. Year 2 scales to a full tri-coastal festival (Seattle + SF Bay Area + Los Angeles).

## Team & Local Context

The founding team is **2-3 people based in Seattle** with **strong existing relationships in the Seattle Indian community**. This is a meaningful strategic asset — it allows faster execution in Seattle, reduces community-engagement friction, and lets the lead spend in-person time at CGI Seattle, venues, and diaspora organizations throughout the 6-month timeline.

## Why Now

Three forces converge:

1. **The India consulate network just doubled its West Coast presence.** CGI Seattle opened in November 2023; a new downtown LA office launched in early 2026. Both are actively courting cultural partners.
2. **The 2024 Seattle University "India Culture Week" is a proven template.** CGI Seattle co-presented a multi-day multi-disciplinary program with a US university and an Indian-origin nonprofit (Roundglass India Center). It sold out. That template is directly reusable.
3. **ICCR's cultural diplomacy budget is flowing into US cities.** In April 2026 ICCR gifted a Swami Vivekananda Monument to Seattle. This is the pattern — ICCR funds and gifts when there is a credible US co-presenter on the receiving end.

## What We Are Asking For

- **From the Consulate(s):** Official endorsement letter, ICCR introduction, co-presenter status, and invitation-letter support for Indian artists' US visa petitions.
- **From ICCR:** Travel grants (round-trip airfare for selected Indian artists).
- **From US funders:** Project funding via PAS Small Grants (US Embassy India), California Arts Council (if LA/SF), 4Culture (if Seattle), and corporate sponsors.
- **From Indian focal sponsor (TBD):** Gap funding for production costs not covered by US grants.

## Critical Constraints

| Constraint | Implication |
|---|---|
| **6-month timeline** | Multi-month grant cycles are largely off the table. Lean on consulate endorsement + corporate + ticket + Indian focal sponsor + ICCR airfare. |
| **No US 501(c)(3) yet** | A new 501(c)(3) cannot be IRS-approved in 6 months. Solution: fiscal sponsorship (Fractured Atlas recommended) OR co-presenter with an existing Indian-arts 501(c)(3). |
| **Festival scale ambition** | Reframe as "Year 1 pilot → Year 2 full festival." More fundable, more credible, more executable. |
| **Bi-coastal scope** | Doubles logistics but strengthens the ICCR/PAS narrative (national impact > single-city event). |

## Recommended Positioning

> *"The inaugural year of an annual India–USA Performing Arts Festival — anchored in Seattle with a flagship Bay Area evening event, co-presented with the Consulate General of India (Seattle and San Francisco), ICCR, and leading US cultural institutions. Expanding to full tri-coastal programming (Seattle + SF Bay Area + Los Angeles) in Year 2."*

## Success Criteria for Year 1 (Pilot)

1. Seattle + California events executed without major incident
2. 3–5 Indian artists/groups brought on P-3 visas with CGI endorsement
3. ICCR Travel Grant or equivalent secured
4. Minimum one major US institutional co-presenter (university, arts center, museum)
5. Press coverage in mainstream and Indian-diaspora media
6. Documented relationships that unlock Year 2 at festival scale

## The Plan, in One Sentence

> In Month 1, leverage in-person access to lock CGI Seattle partnership, fiscal sponsorship, and Seattle community co-presenters; in Month 2 confirm 3–5 Indian artists and submit ICCR + PAS applications; in Month 3 file P-3 visas; in Months 4–5 build out Seattle programming arc and the single Bay Area flagship evening; in Month 6 execute with complete documentation to unlock Year 2.
