## 🇮🇳 Indian Government Programs (for engaging with India → US)

### 1. ICCR Travel Grant for Artists
- **What:** Round-trip economy airfare for Indian citizens performing/presenting abroad
- **Who can use:** Indian citizens — artists, scholars — invited to participate in a cultural event/festival/exhibition abroad
- **How to utilize:**
  1. Secure a formal invitation letter from the US host organization
  2. Apply via [iccr.gov.in](https://iccr.gov.in/online-forms-links/travel-grant/guidelines)
  3. Application is vetted by the local Indian Mission abroad (CGI Seattle or SF)
  4. Endorsement from the Consulate General significantly improves approval odds
- **Covers:** Round-trip airfare ONLY (not visa fees, board, lodging)
- **Timeline:** Apply 8–12 weeks before travel date
- **Contact:** Via CGI Seattle or CGI SF cultural wing

### 2. ICCR Empanelled Artists / Outgoing Cultural Delegations
- **What:** ICCR maintains a pool of empanelled Indian artists across disciplines. When India sponsors cultural delegations abroad, it draws from this pool.
- **Who can use:** The US host organization can request specific empanelled artists via the Consulate; artists apply to ICCR directly to be empanelled
- **How to utilize:** Work through CGI Seattle/SF to request ICCR to send an empanelled delegation to our festival
- **Timeline:** Applications to the list open year-round; delegations require 6+ months lead time

### 3. ICCR Cultural Chairs at Universities
- **What:** ICCR funds "Chairs of Indian Studies" at select universities globally. Current US chairs include several; can be leveraged for academic legitimacy.
- **Relevant for:** Partnering with a university that hosts an ICCR Chair
- **How to utilize:** Identify a US university with an ICCR Chair and propose festival co-presentation

### 4. ICCR Art Residency Programme
- **What:** International artists can apply for short-term residencies in India
- **Secondary relevance:** Not directly useful for bringing Indian artists to US, but establishes a reverse-exchange program that strengthens ICCR narrative
- **How to utilize:** Loop into Year 2 as a sister program (US artists to India, Indian artists to US)

### 5. Lata Mangeshkar Dance & Music Scholarship Scheme (formerly ICCR Scholarship Scheme for Indian Culture)
- **What:** ~100 scholarships globally for foreign students to study Indian classical dance, music, theater, performing arts, sculpture, Indian languages, and cuisine at Indian universities
- **Relevant for:** US-based students we want to train in India (Year 2+ pipeline)
- **Timeline:** Applications typically open February–April annually

### 6. Know India Programme (KIP)
- **What:** Engagement program for people of Indian origin between 18–30, organized by MEA
- **Relevant for:** Connecting US-based Indian-origin youth to India; secondary to our core festival
- **How to utilize:** Pitch a festival volunteer program that ties to KIP alumni

### 7. Government of India Ministry of Culture Schemes
- **What:** Multiple schemes including Guru-Shishya Parampara (honorariums for classical gurus), repertory grants, and financial assistance for cultural events abroad
- **Contact:** [culture.gov.in](https://culture.gov.in/offering/schemes-and-services)
- **Relevant for:** Indian-side funding for participating artists' India-based preparation

## 🇺🇸 U.S. Government Programs (US → India partnerships)

### 8. U.S. Embassy New Delhi — Public Diplomacy Small Grants Program (PAS)
- **What:** Small grants for programs strengthening US-India cultural ties; arts programming and performances eligible
- **Requirement:** Must include an American cultural element or connection with American experts/institutions
- **How to utilize:**
  1. Grants.gov or Embassy New Delhi site for current RFP
  2. Submit proposal with US-India co-programming element
- **Timeline:** Review cycle is typically 3–6 months — challenging but not impossible on our 6-month horizon
- **Contact:** `exchangesIndia@state.gov`

### 9. U.S. Ambassador's Fund for Cultural Preservation (AFCP)
- **What:** Preservation of historic buildings, monuments, museum collections, manuscripts, traditional cultural expressions in India and Bhutan
- **Relevance:** Not directly for our festival, but good to know if we add a cultural-preservation panel or exhibit
- **Contact:** Via US Embassy New Delhi

### 10. Fulbright-Nehru Fellowships (USIEF)
- **What:** Scholarly exchange between US and India
- **Relevance:** Could pair a scholar/artist program with the festival (Year 2)
- **Contact:** [usief.org.in](https://www.usief.org.in)

### 11. Arts Envoy Program (US State Dept.)
- **What:** Sends US artists abroad to foster cross-cultural understanding
- **Relevance:** Year 2 — inverse direction; could enable a US artist to go to India as a festival ambassador
- **Contact:** Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs (ECA), State Dept.

### 12. Young Southeast Asian Leaders Initiative / YES Programs
- **What:** Youth exchange programs
- **Relevance:** Low for us; skip unless adding youth component

### 13. USAGM (US Agency for Global Media) — Cultural Coverage
- **What:** Voice of America, etc. can cover our festival in Hindi and other Indian-language broadcasts
- **Relevance:** PR amplification, not funding

## 🎭 Allied US Cultural Institutions

### 14. National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) — Grants for Arts Projects
- **What:** Project funding in arts categories including Music, Dance, Theater
- **Requirement:** Applicant must be a 501(c)(3) (fiscal sponsorship qualifies for most NEA grants)
- **Timeline:** Annual deadlines; realistically Year 2 funding, not Year 1
- **Contact:** [arts.gov](https://www.arts.gov)
