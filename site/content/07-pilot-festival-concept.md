# Pilot Festival Concept (Year 1)

## Working Title

> **India–USA Performing Arts Festival** *(inaugural pilot year — Seattle-led, with Bay Area flagship)*

Branded as "inaugural," establishing the Year 2 expectation and signaling to funders that this is a multi-year commitment, not a one-off.

---

## Scale & Shape (Seattle-Led Bi-Coastal)

| Dimension | Year 1 Pilot |
|---|---|
| Duration | 4–5 days total |
| Cities | Seattle (primary, 3 days) + SF Bay Area (flagship single evening, 1 day) |
| Performances | 4–5 total (3 in Seattle, 1 flagship in Bay Area) |
| Artists/Groups | 3–5 from India + 2–3 US-based Indian-origin artists |
| Expected audience | ~2,500–4,000 across all events |
| Budget range | $175,000 – $250,000 |
| Format | Multi-disciplinary: classical music, classical dance, and one contemporary/folk element |
| Team capacity | 2–3 person founding team + volunteers from existing Seattle community network |

## Why This Shape Works for a Seattle-Based Small Team

The math on a 2–3 person team running a 6-month festival: **depth in one city beats breadth across two equally.** A Seattle-led shape:

- Concentrates 70% of your operational load in the city you already live in
- Leverages your existing Seattle Indian community relationships for volunteers, community engagement, and word-of-mouth
- Limits Bay Area operational complexity to a single high-impact evening
- Still earns the "national reach" narrative for ICCR, PAS, and federal press coverage

## Artistic Direction

Curate for depth, not breadth. Three principles:

1. **Classical anchor.** At least one Hindustani and one Carnatic tradition represented; at least one classical dance form (Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi, or Kuchipudi).
2. **Cross-generational pairing.** Pair senior masters with rising artists — echoes ICCR's Guru-Shishya Parampara framing and is a strong funder narrative.
3. **One bold contemporary thread.** A fusion piece, a theater work, or a diaspora-collaboration — something that signals "this is India today," not India frozen in amber.

## Artist Curation Framework

Use the following filter when evaluating candidates:

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Established professional performance record in India | Required for P-3 visa "culturally unique" threshold |
| Empanelment with ICCR (if applicable) | Strengthens ICCR travel grant application |
| Availability for a 10–14 day US trip within our window | Festival is 4–5 days; allow travel and rehearsal |
| Willingness to participate in a lecture-demonstration or workshop | Adds educational programming at no extra artist cost |
| Press/awards documentation | Essential for P-3 visa petition |
| Genre spread (not all one discipline) | Ensures festival is multi-disciplinary |
| Cross-generational mix | Senior masters + emerging artists |

## Programming Architecture

**Two parallel tracks across the Seattle days:**

1. **Main stage performances** (ticketed, premium venue, 500–1,200 capacity)
2. **Community programming** (free, university or cultural center venue)
    - Lecture-demonstrations
    - Artist workshops / masterclasses
    - Community Q&A sessions

**The Bay Area evening is ticketed, single-venue, flagship scale** — no community-programming track there. Keep Bay Area simple: one night, one venue, maximum impact, minimum operational drag.

## City-by-City Split

### Seattle (Days 1–3) — The Heart of the Festival

- **Day 1:** Opening gala + headlining classical concert (ticketed)
    - Venue candidates: McCaw Hall, Benaroya Hall, or a mid-size university venue
    - Co-presenter: Seattle University or University of Washington
    - Reception featuring Consul General Gupta, consulate VIPs, sponsors, community partners
- **Day 2:** Lecture-demonstrations at Seattle University or UW (free, public), plus evening dance performance (ticketed, mid-size venue)
- **Day 3:** Community showcase at Seattle Center (free, Festál program or independent booking)
    - Leverages your existing community network for programming collaboration
    - Opportunity to feature local Seattle Indian performing arts groups alongside visiting Indian artists

### Bay Area (Day 4 or 5) — Flagship Evening

- **Flagship concert at a premier Bay Area venue** (ticketed, 1,000–2,000 seats)
    - Primary venue candidate: Center for the Performing Arts, San Jose (CGI SF's proven venue)
    - Secondary candidates: Zellerbach Hall (UC Berkeley), SFJAZZ Center
    - Co-presented with CGI SF
    - Features the flagship Indian artists — top-tier programming, premium format
    - Reception or after-event mixer with Bay Area Indian-American corporate sponsors

## The Narrative Arc

Open with Seattle grandeur. Deepen through Seattle workshop/lec-dem days. Connect with community on Day 3. **Climax in the Bay Area flagship concert.** End with an event that Bay Area corporate sponsors, press, and ICCR will remember.

## What This Shape Makes Possible for a Small Team

1. **All Seattle meetings and walk-throughs are in-person** for the founding team — no travel load
2. **Bay Area requires 2–3 trips total** during the 6 months (Weeks 2, 5, and 20)
3. **Seattle community network carries volunteer and outreach load** — you don't have to build Bay Area community from zero
4. **Bay Area operational complexity is limited to one venue, one night, one audience**
5. **Team of 2–3 can credibly own this scope** without burning out or dropping balls

## What We Are Not Doing in Year 1

- Full multi-day Bay Area programming (Year 2)
- Los Angeles events (Year 2)
- Film screenings (save for Year 2 with LA partnership)
- Major visual arts component (keep performing arts focused)
- A 15+ artist lineup (Year 2 scale)
- International co-programming outside US (out of scope)

## Year 2 Expansion Preview (for pitch narrative)

> *"Year 2 scales to three cities (Seattle + SF Bay Area + Los Angeles), extends to 10–14 days of programming, introduces a film component in LA, and adds a scholars' symposium. Budget scales to $500K+."*

This forward view is essential — funders are writing checks for Year 1 because they see Year 2 coming.
